He Said She Said Super Bowl

January 30, 2014 Originally published in Collegiate Times with companion She Said

You won’t find anything more American than the Super Bowl. A sport that the rest of the world refers to as American football and over-commercialization that would even make Black Friday advertisers blush. It truly has it all.

As sports editor, I am required to enjoy the football aspect the most, but that doesn’t stop me from loving everything that makes the Super Bowl what it is.

Despite the supposed warnings of 1988 cult movie classic “They Live” I see no problems with the over-commercialization of society, especially when it comes to the big productions that are Super Bowl advertisements.

My parents were the founders of the Society for the Commercialization of Christmas, so a love for the gaudy excess of the Super Bowl is inherited.

Speaking of my family, this leads me to my second non-football love of the Super Bowl, the food. My family started a tradition in 2004 by having a big dinner made up of foods local to the teams in the big game.

It’s a great way to try some new foods you might not otherwise and, let’s face it, any excuse to eat way too much delicious food is a good one.

Super Bowl Sunday is essentially a national holiday, just without the time off of work or school. In other words, if you’re watching the game alone, you’ve messed up.

This season the big game is in New York City, or that’s what the National Football League desperately hopes you think. It’s really in a truly depressing place called East Rutherford, NJ.

New Jersey is bad enough on its own, but in the winter it is too cold to be hosting such an important event. If I was made commissioner of the NFL, the game would never leave New Orleans. All national sporting events should be held in New Orleans.

The game itself should be a great one. Both Seattle and Denver are excellent teams and deserve to be playing for it all. We’ve enjoyed a nice run of six straight good, close Super Bowl games and I expect this one to be no different.

So gather some friends and food, pile around the television and celebrate everything that make America great.
